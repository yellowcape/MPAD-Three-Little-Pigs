Once upon a time, there was an old mother pig who had three little pigs...

As they reached the age of 18,

it was tradition to set out on their own to build their homes.

With a mix of pride and bittersweet emotion, the old mother pig bid her children farewell, knowing they were about to embark on a journey of self-discovery and independence.

The first little pig, Bug had a particular love for books. He was often found nestled among stacks of them, his snout buried in tales of adventure and knowledge.

Because of this, he was very lazy and opted to build his house out of straw, preferring the company of his beloved books over manual labour.

The second little pig, Mud, had a passion for cooking, but just like Bug, he was somewhat lazy too.

Despite his culinary talents, Mud often took shortcuts, so instead of fully committing to building his house, he opted for a speedy solution and built his house out of sticks so he could get back to experimenting with new recipes.

The third little pig, Hog, was known for his strong work ethic and his extensive network of friends in construction.

With their help and his own determination, Hog worked hard all day and built his house with bricks. It was a sturdy house complete with a fine fireplace and chimney. It looked like it could withstand even the strongest winds.

The next day, a wolf happened to pass by the lane where the three little pigs lived; and he saw the straw house, and he smelled the pig inside.

He thought the pig would make a mighty fine meal and his mouth began to water.

So he knocked on the door and said:

"Little pig! Little pig! Let me in! Let me in!"

But the little pig saw the wolf's narrow eyes through the keyhole, so he answered back:

"No! No! No!"

Not by the hairs on my chinny chin chin!
So the wolf showed his teeth and said:

Then I'll huff and I'll puff and
I'll blow your house
down.

So he huffed and he puffed and he blew the house down! The wolf opened his jaws very wide and bit down as hard as he could, but the first little pig escaped and ran away to hide with the second little pig.

The wolf continued down the lane and he passed by the second house made of sticks; and he saw the house, and he smelled the pigs inside, and his mouth began to water as he thought about the fine dinner they would make

So he knocked on the door and said:

"Little pig! Little pig! Let me in! Let me in!"

But the little pigs saw the wolf's narrow eyes through the keyhole, so they answered back:

"No! No! No!"

So he huffed and he puffed and he blew the house down! The wolf was greedy and he tried to catch both pigs at once, but he was too greedy and got neither!

His big jaws clamped down on nothing but air and the two little pigs scrambled away as fast as their little hooves would carry them.

The wolf chased them down the lane and he almost caught them. But they made it to the brick house and slammed the door closed before the wolf could catch them. The three little pigs were very frightened, they knew the wolf wanted to eat them. And that was very, very true.

The wolf hadn't eaten all day and he had worked up a large appetite chasing the pigs around and now he could smell all three of them in- side and he knew that the three little pigs would make a lovely feast.

So the wolf showed his teeth and said:

"Then I'll huff and I'll puff and I'll blow your house down!"

Well! he huffed and he puffed. He puffed and he huffed. And he huffed, huffed, puffed, puffed; but he could not blow the house down.

At last, he was so out of breath that he couldn't huff and he couldn't puff anymore. So he stopped to rest and thought a bit.

Suddenly, Hog snapped out of his daydream, his heart racing as he realized the imminent danger. With a surge of adrenaline, he sprang into action, preparing for the wolf's inevitable descent. Disoriented yet alert, Hog surveyed his surroundings, spotting the wolf at the bottom of the chimney with eyes ablaze. With a quickened pulse, Hog prepared for the impending confrontation.

As tension mounted, the wolf lunged forward, but Hog's swift maneuver dodged him. Caught off guard, the wolf hesitated, and in that moment, Hog looked him in the eyes and simply said.

"Wait" Hog's voice was suprisingly calm. "Why don't you join us for a meal, instead?"

Sprised but intruiged, the wolf nodded in agreement.

As Mud stirred his latest culinary creation, a savory aroma filled the air, promising a delicious feast to come.

Meanwhile, outside, Hog and Bug set to work, their hammers and nails crafting a cozy home fit for their unexpected guest.

As the sun dipped below the horizon, the wolf found himself with a new home and newfound friends.

In exchange for their lives, he had gained not only shelter but also acceptance and friendship.

As they gathered around the table to enjoy Mud's delicious meal, laughter and conversation filled the air.

And in that moment, amidst the warmth of friendship and the promise of a brighter tomorrow, they knew that they had found their happily ever after.
